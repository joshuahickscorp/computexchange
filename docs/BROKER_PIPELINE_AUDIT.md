# computexchange: Intake to Settlement Audit + Broker Roadmap

## Bottom line

The hard, un-fakeable spine is already built and proven. The data-parallel render-farm core works end to end: a Postgres FOR-UPDATE-SKIP-LOCKED job queue, straggler hedging with first-commit-cancels-siblings, presigned-S3 chunk I/O, and a typed result merge. The verified-result moat is real, not a slogan: `resultsAgree` (control/verification.go:554) cross-checks redundant workers within a `(hw_class, engine, build_hash)` class with tolerant comparators (embed cosine >= 0.999, classification/json/rerank semantic equality) and byte-exact elsewhere, backed by honeypots, clawback, and quarantine. The Concierge (control/intake.go) genuinely auto-detects a pipeline from a file listing and refuses unknown shapes honestly. The pre-flight quote planner (control/quote.go) is the most under-marketed asset in the repo: it already returns a real cost band, OOM and cold-start risk, confidence, p50/p90 ETA, verification overhead, a bindable quote, AND live supply (`EligibleWorkerCount` / `WarmEligibleWorkerCount` / `EligiblePoolReputation` / `SLAEligible`, quote.go:235-302) using the exact predicate the claim filter uses.

The honest weaknesses are not in the spine; they are in the FUNNEL and the ECONOMY on top of it.

- The funnel leaks. Detection is 100% filename-suffix based (`countExt`, intake.go:86) over 3 narrow patterns. It has a live correctness bug: document-set matches `.pdf` (intake.go:137) but no PDF extractor exists (intake.go:602-615), so a PDF-only repo is detected as supported then yields 0 records. Intake ingress is uncapped (`RawFile` io.ReadAll, intake.go:411), a control-plane OOM vector. And the buyer never sees the quote: the live Launch button POSTs straight to `/v1/pipelines` (web/demo.html:1345) and never calls the built `/v1/quote`, while `max_usd` is silently dropped.
- The economy is cash-only. Pricing is 100% fixed catalogue. There is no spot price discovery, no earn-by-lending credit flywheel (grep confirms no `mint`/`stake`/`barter`; the ledger has exactly 4 cash kinds), and no assembled settlement/exchange surface.

The single biggest unlock is not new capability. It is making the assets you already built VISIBLE and BINDING, then bootstrapping liquidity. Wire the quote into Launch, close the funnel leaks, then stand up the compute-credit flywheel gated by the verification moat. Render and Plane B are real TAM but are correctly deferred behind a verification primitive that does not exist.

## Become the compute broker: the strategy

Own the one thing the incumbents refuse to provide: a VERIFIED clearing layer, bootstrapped on idle Apple Silicon no GPU-cloud can replicate. vast.ai/runpod rent a box and verify nothing; sf-compute brokers reserved H100 blocks; render-network/sheepit verify only renders. None give a buyer a price-it / trust-it / settle-it loop over heterogeneous consumer supply.

1. Price discovery. Phase 1 is surfacing what exists: wire /v1/quote into Launch (cost band, p50/p90 ETA, live supply, pool reputation, SLA flag) and bind the quote to the invoice so the price is a contract. Phase 2 adds a bounded spot index per job_type (discount to fill idle Macs, surge to ration scarce supply), with the catalogue price as the explainable floor. Hard rule: discount the BUYER price while holding `offered_rate_usd_hr` at/above the catalogue floor, because offered_rate is the worker claim hard filter (scheduler.go:450) and a naive discount shrinks the supply you are trying to fill.
2. Credit / liquidity flywheel. Earn-idle / spend-on-jobs compute-credits turn every supplier into a latent buyer and break the two-sided cold-start. Mint at the verified-PASS moment (api.go:1517 outcome != fail), NOT at `released` (which only fires when cash is wired out). Treat the uncollateralized-float problem as a treasury design (a credit is a capped claim on platform take/float), not a ledger clone. Gate issuance behind Connect identity + verified-task history + rate limits, so the verification moat IS the Sybil defense.
3. Heterogeneous-supply brokerage + best execution. Supply no one else can touch: idle consumer Macs, enrollable as fleets, earning only when truly idle. Best execution is the verified result. Ship the supplier-distinct redundancy guard ALONGSIDE fleet enrollment, or a multi-worker supplier becomes its own cross-check peer.
4. Fees / clearing. Keep the take low (1-5%, default 3%); the supplier keep-rate is the supply magnet. Make the per-job settlement a first-class buyer artifact (quoted-vs-actual + verification outcome), assembling data that already exists.
5. Sequenced moves. (i) Make the moat legible (quote-into-Launch + receipt + capacity ticker). (ii) Make the funnel safe and wide (caps + detection fixes + code-repo embed + unified detector). (iii) Grow supply (fleet enrollment + idle detection + same-supplier guard). (iv) Bootstrap liquidity (credit flywheel + spot index). (v) Expand TAM only after the perceptual verification primitive exists.

## The hard problem: verifying non-deterministic work (renders)

The moat is byte-exact or tolerant-semantic cross-worker agreement within a hardware/engine class. That works because every current job type is either deterministic (transcribe, byte-exact) or has a tolerant semantic comparator (embed cosine, classification/json/rerank). A frame render is NEITHER: honest workers on the same input produce per-pixel-different PNGs (DETERMINISM_CLASS.md documents 15% variance from GPU FP non-determinism), so `bytes.Equal` fails and there is no image comparator. Honeypots have the same problem: they need a known answer keyed by `(job_type, input_ref)`, i.e. a canonical golden render, which only exists under a pinned deterministic engine+seed+build.

The only sound path is a NEW perceptual comparator (SSIM / pHash with a tolerance) added to `resultsAgree`, gated by the same `(hw_class, engine, build_hash)` tuple so cross-kernel jitter does not cause wrongful docks. Two hazards must be designed for: (1) a tolerance wide enough to absorb honest jitter is also wide enough to pass a cheater who renders at reduced samples/resolution for a perceptually-close frame and pockets the cost margin - redundancy alone does not catch undersampling, so an explicit anti-undersampling defense is required; (2) decoding image bytes inside the control plane is the first image decode there, a new dependency and a decompression-bomb DoS surface. Until this primitive exists, any render/non-deterministic lane is unverifiable and gameable, and adding it would silently nullify the one moat that beats vast.ai/runpod. Build the comparator BEFORE the lane, never after.

## Rejected / over-hyped

- Sybil-resistant credit issuance gate (standalone). DROP as a work item. It is a guardrail for a mint primitive that does not exist yet; fold the three reusable building blocks (payouts_enabled, reputationTier, the in-flight reservation pattern) into the credit-economy spike when that is greenlit.
- Settlement statements as net-new. Mostly already shipped. Quoted-vs-actual (`JobInvoice`) and the verification outcome (`JobStatus.Verification`) are both already buyer-facing with passing tests; the cited "admin-only" gap is stale. Real residual is a thin UX consolidation (one endpoint/receipt + per-task drill-down) - that is the Wave 1 receipt item, not a separate feature.
- AI-model-artifact detection (run my own model). Detection is a tiny honesty win, but the headline "route to a verifiable batch op against that model" is BLOCKED by the fixed-catalogue, fixed-weights execution layer (hardware.rs advertises 4 hardcoded models; the scheduler hard-filters on `supported_models`; the agent cannot load arbitrary weights). Spike-first at most; do not staff the routing claim.
- Render pattern / streaming-progressive merge / mid-chunk re-chunking as quick wins. Each is gated on machinery that does not exist (a render runner, a partial-commit protocol, per-worker throughput history) or on the verification primitive above. Re-chunking in particular collides with the `DISTINCT ON (chunk_index)` merge and the redundancy/honeypot pairing invariant - it would silently lose data or cross-check coverage. Spike-first, not ship.
- Reverse-auction clearing as an early move. The matching-side ORDER BY is cosmetic because price never flows to money (single catalogue-fixed offered_rate, fixed 90% supplier share). The value-delivering version is a settlement/pricing redesign; let the spot index and credit flywheel land first.

## Honest caveats

- "Wire the quote into Launch" is high-value but NOT near-zero backend: the live button targets `/v1/pipelines`, not `/v1/jobs`, and a single quote prices one job_type while detected pipelines are multi-stage. The advisory band ships now; a binding multi-stage quote is real plumbing.
- Fleet enrollment is a genuine liquidity lever but its "preserves the moat for free" framing is FALSE. Redundancy peer selection excludes only the anchor worker, not the supplier (scheduler.go:232, store.go:1687). Shipping enrollment without the supplier-distinct guard manufactures a self-confirmation hole.
- The credit flywheel's two structural bugs (mint at PASS not at released; uncollateralized float that pays a third worker in cash) make it a treasury-design problem, not a ledger clone. The strategic lane is right; the naive implementation is net-negative.
- The spot index must be per job_type (the global queue/worker counts would surge an embed job because of an unrelated backlog) and must honor the bound-quote price as a ceiling, or it breaks the quote-is-a-contract trust that is the whole point.
- `eligible_now` is a looser superset of the claim filter; surfacing it as "will run your job" backfires for constrained (private_pool / data_residency / elite) jobs. Label it honestly or tighten the count.
- Render and Plane B are real TAM and correctly deferred. The discipline that wins: every expansion preserves the verified-result moat, or it does not ship.
