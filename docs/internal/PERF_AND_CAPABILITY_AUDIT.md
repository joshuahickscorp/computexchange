# computexchange performance and capability audit: the build roadmap, engine decision, and Hawking verdict

## Bottom line

computexchange is a mature, already-tuned stack. The warm model pool, bounded concurrency, hard-filter SKIP-LOCKED claim, straggler hedging, 3-way verification tiebreak, length-bucketed batched prefill (generate_batch), and the OpenAI-Batch API are all shipped, so there is no idle 10x. The real wins are one in-house hot-path optimization that pays for itself (prefix-KV sharing for classification/extraction, ~2-4x on those job types) and two strategic lane plays: wire vLLM as a determinism-gated CUDA serving lane (~3-6x per GPU, within-class verified), and adopt the founder's Hawking engine as the Apple-Silicon continuous-batch lane (its 5.0x-at-B=8 scheduler and its golden-hash determinism harness are exactly the two assets this audit said were worth building, already engineered). Do not build a from-scratch portable runtime; Candle already runs one codebase on Metal and CUDA. Build custom only the determinism harness and the deterministic batch scheduler, and even those are a transplant from Hawking, not a from-zero build.

## Build vs wire: the engine decision

The strategic question is whether computexchange should build its own inference framework. The honest answer is mostly no, with one precise exception.

What is REDUNDANT (do not build):
- A portable CUDA<->Apple-Silicon runtime. Candle already does this. agent/Cargo.toml:44-49 shows the cuda feature mirrors the metal feature verbatim, models::device() is device-agnostic, and the runners are device-agnostic. A from-scratch portable engine would re-solve a solved problem and almost certainly lose to MLX/vLLM on raw speed, which is negative value.
- Most micro-kernel rewrites of the Candle hot path. F16 activations through forward_via_f16 is a likely NET LOSS (it expands Q4 weights to a dense F16 tensor = 4x the native 4-bit traffic, candle quantized/mod.rs:754). The batched-decode GEMM fusion, the megakernel, and ANE offload are all 1.0-1.2x at best and several break byte-exact determinism. ANE is correctly de-scoped: Candle has no ANE backend and FP16 already flipped rerank order (agent/src/runners.rs:408-414).

What to WIRE (best-in-class engines, separate lanes):
- vLLM on CUDA, behind the verification contract, as the nvidia_* serving lane. This is the dominant real win. The Candle CUDA decode path leaves tensor cores idle: the fused SDPA fast path is gated to is_metal() && seq_len==1 (agent/src/quantized_llama_batched.rs:238), so CUDA falls through to a manual q.matmul(k.t())+softmax+matmul (:250-264) on a dequant-to-f32 QMatMul. vLLM's paged-attention + FP16/BF16 tensor-core GEMMs are a real ~3-6x per GPU at greedy/temp=0 (NOT the headline 5-15x; computexchange runs the cheapest sampling and already recovers some prefill batching). Rough cost: ~3-5 person-weeks, of which the runner seam + OpenAI-server shell-out is ~1-2 weeks and the load-bearing remainder is a cross-worker determinism harness.
- MLX as a reputation-trusted (non-redundant) Apple lane only IF a use case demands it. The seam exists (MlxRunner stub, agent/src/runners.rs:1720) but mlx-rs is not a dependency, so this is a from-scratch FFI build (~10-16 weeks) for a marginal ~1.3-2x over computexchange's already-batched Candle path. Lower priority than vLLM, and superseded by porting Hawking (below) for the Apple lane.

What to BUILD custom (the moat, and the ONLY thing worth building):
- A deterministic continuous-batching scheduler and a golden-hash determinism harness. These are the differentiators wired engines cannot sell determinism-gated. But you do not build them from scratch: the founder already built both in Hawking (next section). So even the "build" is a port.

Person-weeks summary: vLLM CUDA lane ~3-5; Hawking determinism harness adoption ~0.5-1; Hawking continuous-batch scheduler port ~4-6; MLX FFI lane ~10-16 (defer); from-scratch portable engine: do not.

De-risking spike order:
1. CUDA determinism soak (FIRST). Stand up two pinned-vLLM nvidia_* workers; prove byte-identical greedy output across A100/H100 SKUs and across restarts. Seed an hw_class-aware honeypot for batch_infer first, because the current honeypot is hw_class-blind (db/schema has no hw_class column; a Candle-seeded answer would byte-fail a correct vLLM result and auto-quarantine an honest worker).
2. Adopt Hawking's golden-hash gate and write the (device, shader_hash) verification-class boundary (no spike needed; pure moat-hardening).
3. Prefix-KV-sharing batched==serial parity proof (Wave 1).

## Hawking: integrate the engine you already built

Hawking is a from-scratch Rust+Metal Apple-Silicon engine (README confirms: no Python, no llama.cpp, no BLAS, no MPSGraph; crates/hawking-core uses metal 0.29 + objc2-metal, zero Candle in Cargo.lock; no Go, no CUDA). It is Apple-Silicon ONLY, so it never touches the cloud lane, and it is pre-proof (baseline ~31 dec tok/s on M3 Pro; headline wins are M3-Pro-measured, the B=16 and big-model bands are unvalidated). Weigh it honestly: its kernels are NOT the asset (Hawking is ~0.62x llama.cpp at batch-1 decode per docs/dead_levers.md, and its spec-decode is net-negative on the tested Mac). Its DETERMINISM DISCIPLINE is the asset, and that discipline IS computexchange's verification moat, already engineered.

Adopt in this order:

1. The golden-hash determinism harness FIRST (S, ~0.5-1 person-week, PROVEN). crates/hawking-core/tests/golden/*.hashes are token-baseline hashes that gate cross-version byte-identity within a (device, shader_hash) class. The profile system already keys identity on device_name + shader_hash + tensor_layout_hash. This is the cheap moat-hardening: lift the gate concept into computexchange and define the verification class so redundancy peers are pinned to a (device, shader_hash) class. This directly discharges the audit's "build the determinism harness" recommendation; Hawking is the source.

2. The continuous-batching scheduler SECOND (L, ~4-6 person-weeks, PROVEN at B=8 on M3 Pro). crates/hawking-serve/src/batch/scheduler.rs (group_by_prefix, prefill_slots_prefix_grouped with min_shared=8) + driver.rs (decode_ready_once with greedy-vs-sampled lane routing: B*4-byte token-only readback when all-greedy, full B*vocab logits otherwise) + slot-strided KV (a slot keeps its KV region as the ready set churns). Measured 5.0x aggregate vs single-stream at B=8. This is the genuine differentiator wired engines do not sell determinism-gated. Port it as a SEPARATE Apple-only continuous-batch lane, re-gated cross-worker. Discharges the audit's "build the deterministic batch scheduler" recommendation; Hawking is the source.

3. RWKV-7 SSM lane THIRD (L, capability bet, kernels PROVEN, instruct quality Studio-gated). crates/hawking-core/shaders/rwkv7.metal + rwkv_decode_arena.rs deliver O(1)-in-context state and flat decode tps (119.85->117.88 tok/s from short to 8K, vs the transformer KV wall). MLX literally cannot run RWKV-7. The kernels are largely done; the gating cost is the deferred instruct post-train + a per-bit quality card. Capability moat, not a speed headline.

4. F16 KV cache as an opt-in long-context lane (M). HAWKING_QWEN_F16_KV gives -50% KV footprint, ~0% short-ctx tps, scales to ~15% at 16K. Critically it is 88% argmax-identity, NOT byte-identical, which is why Hawking keeps it default-OFF to preserve the golden path. In computexchange it must be a separate non-byte-exact lane or it breaks generation verification.

DO NOT pull in (yet): spec decode (Hawking's own benches show n-gram τ=1.43 best but still sub-gate, and the trained EAGLE head is net-negative 0.40x-0.21x on Qwen-3B+code, docs/dead_levers.md:44); the megakernel (a skeleton in a Type-1-dead dispatch-overhead region by Hawking's own kill ledger); the sub-Q4/condense codec (every requant-from-quantized form NO-GO; AWQ-from-f16 held at 1.115x, below ship gate).

The hard Apple-only boundary and the decisive caveat: Hawking's own research concludes token-level determinism is IMPOSSIBLE across heterogeneous GPUs ("semantic replay, not bit-exact", docs/plans/research/i_trust_verifiability.md:25, citing up to 15% accuracy variance across runs at temp=0). The golden-hash harness proves byte-identity WITHIN a (device, shader_hash) class, not ACROSS Mac generations. So adopting Hawking does not magically give cross-Mac byte-equality; it gives you the machinery to DEFINE and ENFORCE the class boundary the redundancy market must respect.

## The build roadmap

### Wave 1 (ship-now, high impact / low-medium effort)

- Prefix-KV sharing for classification/extraction (M, ~2-3 wk). Prefill the shared instruction+label-list/schema prefix's KV ONCE, fork per item. classification_prompt (agent/src/runners.rs:1298) and extraction_prompt (:1405) put the fixed instruction first and the variable item last, so the shared tokens are a true contiguous prefix; the KV cache is forkable (per-layer (b_sz, n_kv_head, seq_len, head_dim), compact_kv_cache already index_selects on dim 0). Revised impact: ~2-4x batch_classification, ~1.5-2.5x json_extraction; 0x batch_infer/rerank, and toward the low end on a bandwidth-bound 1B model unless the prefix:item ratio is large. Determinism: needs_care; verification compares LABEL/canonical-JSON not bytes (verification.go classificationAgree/jsonExtractionAgree), so it survives, but re-prove the batched==serial parity test (runners.rs:2941) for the prefix-shared path.
- Adopt Hawking's golden-hash gate + define the (device, shader_hash) verification class (S, ~0.5-1 wk). Source: crates/hawking-core/tests/golden/*.hashes. Determinism: this IS the determinism work; safe by construction (it changes no outputs, it records the tolerance boundary).
- Length-bucket the embedder/reranker (S, ~2-4 days). embed_texts (agent/src/runners.rs:603) is the single shared path so one change fixes embed AND rerank. Reuse the generate_batch bucketing (runners.rs:1019-1031). Revised impact: 1.15-1.6x on length-skewed chunks, ~0 on uniform. Determinism: needs_care in implementation, not math; rerankAgree demands EXACT order-array equality and meanCosine pairs BY POSITION, so the bucketing must sort deterministically with a stable tie-break and scatter outputs back to original index (exactly what generate_batch does). A botched scatter-back breaks the market.

### Wave 2 (bigger bets)

- Wire vLLM as the CUDA serving lane (L->XL, ~3-5 wk). InferenceBackend::Vllm + VllmRunner shelling to a pinned vLLM OpenAI server, greedy/temp=0, claiming batch_infer/classification/json/rerank. Mirror the MlxRunner seam (agent/src/runners.rs:1706-1759), insert-first at main.rs:570, reuse sandbox.rs run_sandboxed. Revised impact: ~3-6x batch_infer per GPU (NOT 5-15x). Determinism: needs_care but FAVORABLE; nvidia_* is a distinct hw family never cross-compared with Apple (control/types.go:32-34), so the contract is vLLM-vs-vLLM byte-equality, achievable with pinned engine+dtype. Gate: hw_class-aware honeypot seeding (the honeypot is hw_class-blind today) and a within-tier byte-stability soak across SKUs/restarts.
- Port Hawking's continuous-batching scheduler as the Apple-Silicon lane (L, ~4-6 wk, Apple-only). Source: crates/hawking-serve/src/batch/{scheduler.rs,driver.rs} + slot-strided KV in qwen_dense.rs. Revised impact: ~5.0x aggregate vs single-stream at B=8 (measured in Hawking). Determinism: needs_care; the B-dependent kernel routing means batched output is not bit-identical to solo (parity tests assert atol=1e-3, not bit-identity), but this is the SAME cross-hardware fp16 risk the single-stream path already carries, not a new risk class. Re-gate cross-worker on pinned hardware class.
- Preallocate the KV cache via slice_set append (L for the batched path, M for bsz==1). Replace the per-step Tensor::cat (agent/src/quantized_llama_batched.rs:230-231) with candle-nn's already-shipping KvCache (preallocated append via slice_set). Revised impact: ~1.05-1.15x on 256-token jobs; main value is as the prerequisite for ragged batching. Determinism: safe (slice_set writes the same bytes at a position offset, bitwise-identical logits).
- Schedule on measured throughput as a claim-time tiebreak (S). Add a bw_gbps/tps ORDER BY term below the existing pin+priority+cheaper-class terms in ClaimTask. Revised impact: low-single-digit to ~10% mean latency on a heterogeneous fleet; ~0 on uniform; the 10-30% top end is not credible for a low-priority tiebreak. Determinism: safe (selection only, no output change).

### Wave 3 (frontier / spike-first)

- RWKV-7 SSM long-context lane (L, capability). Source: crates/hawking-core/shaders/rwkv7.metal, rwkv_decode_arena.rs. Flat ~118 tps at 8K. Determinism: needs_care (Hawking flagged it determinism_sensitive:false, but rwkv7_metal_parity uses LOGIT_TOL=0.05 and the recurrent state cascades a single argmax flip irreversibly; a TQ integer-deterministic path exists but is off-by-default and only same-device-validated). Prereq: the deferred instruct post-train + a quality card.
- Constrained/grammar (JSON-schema) decoding in the batched serve path (M for core, L for user grammars). Source: Hawking's JsonConstraint/JsonVocabIndex (single-stream only today) into the batch lane; user-supplied schema/regex lives in hawking-orch and needs crate-edge work. Capability unlock, not throughput.
- F16 KV cache as an opt-in long-context lane (M). -50% KV footprint; MUST be a separate non-byte-exact lane (88% argmax-identity).
- vLLM/larger-model band on high-VRAM nvidia_80g/180g (M, capability). Add 13B/32B/70B to the catalogue once the vLLM lane lands; the memory gate already exists.

## Capability frontier

- CUDA cloud lane via vLLM: ~3-6x batch_infer per GPU, plus the path to a 13B/32B/70B band only the CUDA lane can serve (the current cap is Qwen2.5-7B, models.rs is_big_llama keyed on a 7b marker). Prereq: vLLM wired + within-tier determinism soak + hw_class-aware honeypots.
- Apple-Silicon continuous batching (Hawking port): 5.0x aggregate at B=8 on the Apple lane, the differentiator no wired engine sells determinism-gated. Prereq: golden-hash class boundary defined first.
- RWKV-7 long-context: a flat-tps, O(1)-state long-context product MLX cannot offer at all. Prereq: instruct post-train + quality card.
- Structured/agentic output under load: grammar-constrained batched decode unlocks tool-use/agent workloads. Prereq: port JsonConstraint into the batch lane.
- Bigger models on unified memory (Apple) is a CAPACITY product (this 70B job CAN run here), priced on fit not tok/s; the routing primitive (min_memory_gb hard-filter) already exists, but the underlying 30-70B Apple runner is unbuilt, so this is gated on a real large-model runner, not on scheduling.

## Rejected / over-hyped

- F16 activations via forward_via_f16 (subsystem A): likely NET LOSS. Expands Q4 weights to dense F16 = 4x native 4-bit traffic per token. Only the F16-KV slice is real, and it is a memory win, not the claimed 1.3-1.7x. Breaks generation byte-equality.
- Batched-decode GEMM fusion / mv->mm (A): real but ~1.2-1.6x at B>=8, and breaks determinism (mm's F16-tiled accumulation differs from mv's per-row F32 sum, flipping greedy argmax vs the B=1 path that seeds honeypots).
- Whisper batching / O(n^2) fix (A): mis-diagnosed; the upstream whisper has no self-attn cache to flush, so it is an L model fork for ~1.2-1.5x, not 2-4x.
- From-scratch portable engine (A/C/D): redundant; Candle already runs Metal+CUDA.
- Megakernel (Hawking G): a no-op skeleton in a Type-1-dead dispatch-overhead region by Hawking's own kill ledger; uses f16 weights (2x the bandwidth of production Q4_K), so it would be slower. Only defensible action is delete-and-record.
- Spec decode (Hawking frontier): net-negative on the tested Mac (EAGLE head 0.40x-0.21x; n-gram τ=1.43 best but sub-gate). Needs a new batched multi-query Metal kernel that does not exist; not "wiring an existing seam".
- int4-KV into the multi-seq hot path (frontier): blocked (per-channel kernels are single-sequence; the multiseq batched int4 kernel does not exist) and B>8 is independently capped by 1..=8-asserting GEMMs; breaks determinism (per-layer int4 error compounds over 36 layers).
- Greedy-aware mixed-lane partitioning (frontier): the "150000x readback" cost model is wrong on unified memory (logits readback is a pointer deref, ~4.6 MB at B=8); splitting the batch doubles the dominant ~4.5 GB weight read. Near-zero to negative.
- Two malformed/placeholder proposals (B-concurrency, E-control, one F-capability) had no content; drop.
- Several proposals (scheduler bw tiebreak's verification, custom GPU-second meter, CUDA flash-attn, big-model CUDA tier, RunPod image) were submitted against the WRONG repo path (the cwd was Hawking, not computexchange) and are unverifiable as written; the bw-tiebreak and GPU-second meter are plausible if re-grounded.

## Determinism ledger

SAFE (no output change, ship without re-proof):
- Adopt golden-hash gate / define verification class (it IS the determinism work).
- KV preallocation via slice_set (writes identical bytes at offset).
- Schedule on bw_gbps tiebreak (selection only).
- vLLM CUDA lane within nvidia_* (distinct hw family, never cross-compared with Apple; vLLM-vs-vLLM byte-equality with pinning).

NEEDS-CARE (real risk, gateable):
- Prefix-KV sharing: label/JSON verification is tolerant, but re-prove batched==serial parity.
- Embedder bucketing: math drifts ~1e-6 but peers run identical bucketing; the risk is a botched stable-sort + scatter-back against exact-order rerank verification.
- Hawking continuous-batch port: B-dependent kernel routing is not bit-identical to solo (atol=1e-3); same cross-hw fp16 risk the status quo already carries; re-gate on pinned class.
- vLLM honeypots: hw_class-blind today; seed hw_class-aware honeypots before go-live.

BREAKS DETERMINISM (separate non-redundant lane only, or do not ship):
- F16 activations through forward_via_f16; mv->mm GEMM fusion; F16 KV (88% argmax-identity); int4-KV multiseq; MLX/vLLM continuous batching with dynamic batch composition for byte-exact job types.

The governing truth: token-level determinism is impossible across heterogeneous Mac generations (Hawking's own research). The market must pin redundancy peers to a (device, shader_hash) class or compare with tolerance, never byte-equality across Mac microarchitectures. This boundary is the FIRST thing to encode.

## Honest caveats

- Hawking is Apple-Silicon ONLY. It never touches the CUDA cloud lane. The CUDA wins come exclusively from vLLM.
- Hawking is pre-proof. The 5.0x-at-B=8 and flat-RWKV numbers are M3-Pro-measured; B=16, the big-model band, paged-KV, and the instruct-quality of the SSM model are unvalidated. The headline continuous-batch win is real at B=8; "6-8x" extensions are spike-gated and the project's own ceiling oracle self-warns it overpredicts ~2.5-4x.
- The vLLM ~3-6x is the realistic decode/throughput band at greedy/temp=0 and chunk-bounded concurrency, NOT vLLM's headline serving numbers. The dominant gain is tensor-core GEMM + paged-attention replacing the dequant-to-f32 manual CUDA decode, and it is gated entirely on the determinism soak, not on the FFI.
- Prefix-KV sharing's 2-4x is on classification/extraction only and trends to the low end on bandwidth-bound 1B models with small prefix:item ratios.
- The big-model unified-memory "capacity product" has no underlying Apple runner today (cap is 7B). The routing/pricing scaffolding exists; the runner does not. Do not market a band that is unbuilt.
- The sub-Q4 byte-cut codec prize (+31/71% bandwidth) is the largest single lever on paper but is unclaimed: every requant-from-quantized form is NO-GO and AWQ-from-f16 is held below the ship gate. Treat as a Wave-3 spike with a sizable NO-GO branch, not a fundable build.
